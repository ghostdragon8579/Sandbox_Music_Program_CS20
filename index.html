This is a free-to-use music program designed and published for CS20. (Computer Science 20-1) It was made as a more advanced replacment for the music program I developed in CS10 (Computer Science 10-1) It contains a few songs paired with images that are designed to accurately depict the music that is currently selected It also contains a progress indicator for the music that is selected along with various buttons to skip, rewind, and pause the selected music.

This is a rough draft of the design that will be used in the music player created for CS20. The focus of the design is to create a simple interface that is easy to learn how to use. The following is a summary of the design's various buttons and their purposes:

  • The button on the far left skips to the previous song in the playlist.
  • The button second from the left rewinds the selected song by ten seconds.
  • The middle button plays the selected song however if the song is already playing it pauses the song instead.
  • The button second from the right skips ten seconds ahead in the selected song.
  • The button on the far right skips to the next song in the playlist.
  • The button with an X on it in the top right of the design quits the program.

    • This is the color scheme that will be used in the music player created for CS20. The colors are designed to be noticeable and visually appealing. The following is a description of the various aspects of the color scheme:

  • The overall theme is going to be a neon design mainly made up of purple and black.
  • The background of the music player will be neon purple border.
  • The text that will be used in the music player will the same color as the border.
  • The buttons that will be used to manipulate the music that is selected will be an combination of black and neon white.
  • However the exit button will not follow this color scheme with the colors composing it being red and black.

    • The music player is designed to be used to play background music while the user is doing something else. The first of the presented videos accurately represents the kind of background music I imagine this music player would be used for. However due the music that is played is entirely dependent on the user something more like the second video displays may be played instead.
